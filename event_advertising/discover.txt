Join LeeAnn Latremouille from Birds Canada for a guided driving tour of some wetlands outside Brandon! This is part of the field component of the MarshWatch webinar series; it’s a great way to apply what you learned (or a great way to learn about the program itself if you haven’t joined before).

Birds Canada is also hosting walks at Oak Hammock Marsh on July 26th and Spruce Woods Provincial Park on July 26th and 27th. More details on the MarshWatch program can be found on [their website](https://www.birdscanada.org/bird-science/marshwatch/prairiecanada-marshwatch).

Saturday, Aug 15 2026 - 9:00 AM
East of Neepawa

Westman Naturalists - Langford Trails

Hike the Langford Trail system by Neepawa with us! Depending on the weather and how much walking you feel like doing, there are options to go 1.5km, 3.5km, or 6.5km. Last year we saw lots of chipmunks and heard quite a few birds in the trees!

There are some ups and downs on the trails, so please bring proper footwear. There may also be Poison Ivy at the site. Consider bringing a mid-hike snack, there are a few benches along the hike to stop at and take in the scenery!

Saturday, Aug 29 2026 - 9:00 AM
South of Wawanesa

Westman Naturalists - Noble Lake

Noble Lake is a Manitoba Habitat Conservancy property south of Wawanesa. It's a great spot for botany and birding, and at this time of year we can hope to catch some birds on migration. Later-blooming wildflowers and different grasses are sure to be plentiful, they can't go anywhere else.

Be sure to bring any books or apps to help with identification, and your binoculars or camera if you have them! The walk will be along mowed paths and some rougher trails through forested areas, likely no more than 2km. We may also make a couple stops while driving to get a different view of the lake and other hotspots.

**Participation**: Please complete this [short form](https://forms.gle/UPqMr2gFbqgXqAkP7) to sign-up for this event. We will email you with specific details a day or so before the event. Car pooling might be available.

Saturday, Sep 12 2026 - 9:00 AM
North of Virden

Westman Naturalists - Autumn Hawk Watch

Join us for our annual autumn hawk watch along the river valley north of Virden. Starting at 9am, take a seat (lawn chairs recommended) and watch the skies for raptors and any other birds making their way south for fall migration. Hawks/eagles/falcons and such are the goal, but we often see a lot of waterfowl and songbirds travelling too, if the wind is favourable!

We will have telescopes and binoculars available, but please bring yours if you have them. Bring a chair, too, as we'll be staying in one spot till around noon (sometimes later, depending on the birds). Snacks, water, and a lunch are always a good idea!

**Participation**: Please complete this [short form](https://forms.gle/MSRbLnp4A6Pceka2A) to sign-up for this event. We will email you with specific details a day or so before the event. Car pooling might be available.
