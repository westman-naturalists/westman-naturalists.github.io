Westman Naturalists - MarshWatch Prairie Tour with Birds Canada

Join LeeAnn Latremouille from Birds Canada for a guided driving tour of some wetlands outside Brandon! This is part of the field component of the MarshWatch webinar series; it’s a great way to apply what you learned (or a great way to learn about the program itself if you haven’t joined before).

We'll meet at the northeast corner of the [Victoria Inn parking lot](https://maps.app.goo.gl/63t7eiQLz2BQ3S7n8), before heading out for at least a couple hours. Bring your binoculars if you have them, or we have a couple extra that can be shared. As always, dress for the weather!

Birds Canada is also hosting walks at Oak Hammock Marsh on July 26th and Spruce Woods Provincial Park on July 26th and 27th. More details on the MarshWatch program can be found on [their website](https://www.birdscanada.org/bird-science/marshwatch/prairiecanada-marshwatch).

Westman Naturalists - Wednesday Walk at the Discovery Centre

On August 12th we'll be walking around the Brandon Riverbank Discovery Centre! Always a great place for a walk, be on the lookout for a variety of wildlife including prairie and marsh birds, snakes, frogs, and mammals. The walk will be around 1.5km over a well-maintained path.

Meet at the [west end of the parking lot](https://maps.app.goo.gl/9t9xQzwt43AUv11q6). Look for people with binoculars or cameras!

Due to flooding this year, the trails may look a bit different. We'll update this event closer to the actual date in case any details need to be changed.

**Participation**: No sign up is required for this event, though there will be a waiver to sign.

Westman Naturalists - Wednesday Walk at Bison Park in Minnedosa

Join us for a walk around Bison Park and along the river in Minnedosa! The walk will be just under 3km, with a steeper section when we go to cross the dam. We should see plenty of birds and probably some mammals too (such as the bison, in the park).

Westman Naturalists - Wednesday Walk at the Assiniboine Food Forest

It's our last evening walk of the season! Join us at the Assiniboine Food Forest for a walk along the river and through the trees. We will be walking up to about 2km on the trails. American Red Squirrels are always chattering in the forest, lots of White-tailed Deer frequent the area, and we may see some migrating birds!

We'll meet at [Crow's General Store in the parking lot](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tDMiGhipQc2xJ3z56). After the walk, treat yourself to some ice cream!

Due to flooding this year, the trails may look a bit different. We'll update this event closer to the actual date in case any details need to be changed.
